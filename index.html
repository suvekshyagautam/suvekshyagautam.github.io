Hello! I'm a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Washington - Seattle, specializing in Development Economics. My research interests focus on understanding economic inequalities, labor market outcomes, financial inclusion, and women's empowerment.

During my doctoral studies, I've worked as a Graduate Research Assistant on several impactful projects, including studying village-based livelihood programs, mobile money's effect on female microfinance clients in Tanzania, and COVID-19's welfare impacts on garment workers in Bangladesh.

With 7+ years of experience conducting field research, impact evaluations, and data analysis across low- and middle-income countries, I'm passionate about addressing pressing socioeconomic challenges, particularly those affecting vulnerable populations and developing economies.

Research

Social Exclusion and Risk Sharing: Evidences from Nepal
Status: Work in Progress

Using household-level panel data, this study examines how social networks mediate income risks and how exclusion based on caste leads to differing levels of risk-sharing across community members. The findings reveal that socially excluded groups face greater vulnerability to income shocks, as their access to risk-sharing networks is limited.

Financial Exclusion Social Networks Consumption Smoothing
Mobile money and women's empowerment: a lab-in-the-field experiment
With Emma Riley
Status: Work in Progress

This research investigates how access to mobile money services affects women's economic empowerment, financial decision-making, and household bargaining power through a lab-in-the-field experiment in developing contexts.

Financial Inclusion Gender Economics Field Experiment
Women's job market opportunities and marriage quality
With Rachel M. Heath and Jagori Chatterjee
Status: Work in Progress

This study investigates how improvements in women's labor market opportunities affect household dynamics, decision-making power, and overall marriage quality in developing countries.

Gender Economics Labor Markets Household Dynamics
Impact of in-person versus phone assessments on students' performance
With Uttam Sharma, Shwetlena Sabarwal, and Karthika Radhakrishnan
Status: Work in Progress

This research compares the effectiveness and accuracy of in-person versus phone-based student assessment methods, with implications for education monitoring in resource-constrained and remote settings.

Education Assessment Remote Learning Methodology
Course Corrections? The Labor Market Returns to Correctional Education Credentials
With Dan Goldhaber and James Cowan
Status: Revise and Resubmit

This paper examines the labor market returns to education credentials earned while incarcerated, using administrative data and rigorous causal inference methods to estimate effects on post-release employment outcomes.

Education Economics Labor Market Econometrics
Assessing the Academic Mobility on Test and Non-test Outcomes for Students from Third Grade through High School
With Dan Goldhaber and Stephanie Liddle
Status: Work in Progress

This project examines how student mobility affects both standardized test performance and non-test outcomes like attendance, behavior, and graduation rates through longitudinal analysis of comprehensive education data.

Status: Work in Progress
