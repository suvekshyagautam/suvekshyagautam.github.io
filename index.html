About Me

I am a development economist currently pursuing my PhD in Economics at the University of Washington with a focus on Development Economics and Econometrics. My research interests center on financial inclusion, gender economics, education policy, and labor markets in low and middle-income countries.

With over 7 years of experience, I have conducted field research and impact evaluations across multiple countries, developing expertise in experimental design, econometric analysis, and policy-relevant research. I am passionate about using rigorous empirical methods to address development challenges and inform evidence-based policies.

I have collaborated with academic institutions, multilateral organizations like UNESCO, and non-profit research centers. My work bridges academic research and practical policy applications, with a commitment to improving economic opportunities and outcomes in developing contexts.
